Alert Engine

Seventeen
spatial
proximity checks

Seventeen spatial proximity checks that run entirely in the browser against data already loaded in memory, with no server-side processing required. These are heuristic flags, not validated findings.

All alert logic is client-side only, documented in plain language within the app itself, and designed for field review by practitioners who can identify false positives or missing conditions.
PFAS
PFAS Near Habitat

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substance (PFAS) contamination site within 1 km of any corridor node or Monarch Watch Waystation (Haversine distance).

Habitat
Unsupported Sites

Corridor sites with no iNaturalist or GBIF pollinator sighting recorded within 500 m.

Habitat
Opportunity Zones

~1 km grid cells with ≥5 sightings and no formal habitat designation within 800 m — restoration candidates.

Habitat
Public Land Gap

City/County parcel ≥0.5 ac inside an opportunity zone with no nearby habitat — parcels that may be worth a closer look.

Habitat
Declining Public Zone

Opportunity zone with a declining sighting trend and publicly owned land nearby.

Connect
Connected Pairs

Habitat site pairs within 300 m — corridor stepping-stone distance.

Connect
Isolated Habitat

Habitat site with no other registered habitat within 2 km, based on loaded data sources.

Connect
Weak Nodes

Corridor sites whose nearest neighbor is 700 m–2 km away, indicating a weakly connected network position.

Connect
Connectivity Gap

Widest edge in the corridor's minimum spanning tree — flagged when it exceeds 2.5 km.

Connect
Quadrant Coverage

Four quadrants around map centroid; flagged when any quadrant has no designated habitat.

Crops
Pollinator–Crop Mismatch

Bee-dependent crop acreage in areas without adequate estimated habitat coverage nearby.

Site
High Pesticide Pressure

Corridor sites in top-quartile pesticide-pressure counties per the Critical Band index.

Site
Poor Nesting Habitat

Sites scoring below 25/100 on the National Land Cover Database (NLCD)-derived nesting suitability index.

Site
Shaded Habitat

Corridor sites with >55% tree canopy coverage within 150 m (WI DNR Urban Tree Canopy 2022).

Climate
Growing Degree Days (GDD) Below Normal

Daily growing-degree accumulation (base 50°F) running below the 30-year normal for the current May–September window at Green Bay. GDD is computed from IEM observed temperature data and does not require the NOAA optional key.

Climate
Fall Frost Risk

At least one sub-34°F forecast within the next 14-day NOAA window during the active pollinator season (May–Sep). Late frosts threaten late-season milkweed and nectar resources. Requires NOAA optional key.

Data
Temporal Mismatch

A loaded data layer whose vintage year is more than 3 years old while live-rendered on the map. Flagged to prompt users to verify whether older data is still representative of current conditions.

Every layer is sourced from publicly available APIs and datasets. No proprietary data, no lock-in, no dependency on any single organization's goodwill. The only thing Bay Hive adds is the assembly — putting it in one place for anyone working on pollinator habitat in this region.

No proprietary datasets required for core functionality. Optional keys (eBird, NASS, NOAA) unlock additional layers; Bay Hive degrades gracefully without them. Climate normals and observed temperatures load from bundled and IEM sources — the Climate Ribbon works without a NOAA key. Observation data cached 1 hour · Area layers cached 24 hours · Cache/live status visible in the intelligence bar.
